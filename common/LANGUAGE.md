# Language Guidance

Use this file for shared word choice and sentence-level edits.

## Prefer

* Concrete nouns and named actors.
* Strong verbs over noun-heavy phrasing.
* Direct openings over throat-clearing.
* Specific, qualified claims over broad assertions.
* Reasons and criteria over cue words.
* Common words unless precision requires a technical term.

## Avoid

* Unsupported hype.
* Filler such as "great question", "it is important to note", and "in today's world".
* Apology padding unless an apology is needed.
* Decorative metaphors and transitions.
* Weak intensifiers such as "very", "really", "quite", "somewhat", "basically", and "kind of".
* Unqualified rankings.
* Certainty words that outrun the evidence.

## Sentence Checks

* Name the actor unless the actor is unknown or irrelevant.
* Use active voice when it is clearer or shorter.
* Use positive phrasing when rejection or negation obscures the point.
* Make parallel ideas grammatically parallel.
* Keep related words close together.
* Split paragraphs when the topic changes.
* Keep the sharper sentence when two sentences make the same point.
